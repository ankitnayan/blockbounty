BlockBounty: Blockchain based bug bounty platform

Many a times, when bounty hunters report bugs on bounty platforms, the client comes back and says that they already know about the bug and are working on it. This is a huge problem for bounty hunters, as they don’t have a way to counter the client - and basically need to agree with the client.

How blockchain will solve the problem of clients saying that the bug is duplicate and they are already working on it??

BlockBounty is a bug bounty platform which enables transparency between clients and bounty hunters using cryptographic proof and immutability of blockchain.

How it works?

  • When a client is working on a bug, he puts the hash of the bug report on blockchain.
  • When a bounty hunter shares a bug pointing to the same issue, the client will share the hash of the bug report on blockchain
  • The actual bug report will not be shared by the client unless the bug is fixed Once the bug is fixed, the client will share the original bug report
  • Once the bug is fixed, the client will share the original bug report
  • The bounty hunter can create a hash of the bug report and check if it matches with the hash pointed by the client - and published earlier.
  • If the hash doesn’t match, he can dispute clients claim (and be paid the bounty automatically)
  • If the hash does match, the bounty hunter can still choose to dispute - but in this case the issue will be referred to a decentralised jury. (Refer to decentralised jury design)
  • The decentralised jury is a set of trusted voters who have staked some token in the network. (We assume that greater than 51% of the jury members are honest)

Decentralised Jury Design

  • Each jury member will have to stake certain amount.
  • They vote on the question:
    Is the bug report shared by the client and that by the bounty hunter point to the same bug or not?
  • They vote in Yes or No.
  • Each member vote in a secret manner They majority decision is considered as valid.
  • The members voting against the majority are penalised from their staked amount.
